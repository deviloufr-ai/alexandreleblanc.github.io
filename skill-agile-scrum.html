How I work

Agile / Scrum

Process that serves the team — not the other way around.

My philosophy

Agile is a mindset, not a compliance exercise. The worst teams follow ceremonies perfectly and miss the point entirely. The goal is fast learning loops, not process adherence. I adapt the framework to context: a blockchain infrastructure team has different rhythms than an AdTech product team or a hardware IoT team. The principles are constant; the implementation varies.

Sprint setup

Two-week sprints. Sprint goal written before backlog items are selected. Definition of Done includes: code reviewed, tests written, documentation updated, analytics event live, deployed to staging — and for blockchain products: testnet validation passed, security checklist reviewed. No DoD exceptions without explicit team agreement. At Datachain this DoD prevented 3 potential mainnet incidents caught in testnet validation.

Lean ceremonies

Planning: 90 min max, goal-first. Daily standup: 15 min, three questions only. Refinement: 60 min/week, async pre-reading required. Review/demo: 45 min, real stakeholders present when possible. Retro: 45 min, action items owned and tracked. Total overhead: ~5 hours per 2-week sprint. I cut any ceremony that doesn't change a decision.

Scrum Master at Datachain

As Scrum Master for the IBC bridge program at Datachain, my focus was impediment removal, not meeting facilitation. I kept a live impediment log, reviewed it daily, and measured my performance by blocker resolution speed. Average resolution time: under 48 hours. The most complex blockers were cross-team: Engineering waiting on QA capacity, or SRE waiting on an external chain's API stability — these required escalation, negotiation, and creative sequencing, not just process.

Scaling Agile across teams

At SmartNews I applied Agile processes across 5 Ads product teams simultaneously using a lightweight Scrum-of-Scrums: one cross-team sync per week — top blocker and upcoming dependency per team. No status reports, only decisions and blockers. Team velocity increased 25% over 2 quarters. At Hakuhodo, Agile adoption reduced average feature delivery time by 40% for the Pechat hardware/software release cycle — a hardware context where sprint-level iteration is harder than in pure software.
