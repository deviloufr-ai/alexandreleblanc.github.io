Go-to-Market

Launching products that land — hardware, software, games, and protocols.

GTM is a product problem

Most GTM failures are positioning failures or product-market fit failures dressed up as marketing failures. I treat GTM as a product workstream starting at ideation: who is this for, what job does it do, why us, why now. In Web3, this is even more critical — a protocol launch without clear positioning in an ecosystem gets ignored regardless of technical quality.

The GTM brief

Before any launch I produce a one-page GTM brief: target segment + persona, core value proposition (one sentence), key differentiators (max 3), channels and owner per channel, success metrics at 30/60/90 days, and risk register. Every function — Engineering, Marketing, BD, Legal — signs off before launch date is set.

Web3 / Protocol GTM

Protocol and DeFi product GTM has unique elements: ecosystem developer relations (convincing other protocols to integrate), community launch strategy (Discord, Twitter/X, Telegram), token/incentive design impact on adoption, and coordinated security disclosure if any vulnerabilities are found pre-launch. At Datachain, the IBC bridge GTM focused on banking and institutional partners — a B2B enterprise motion layered on top of a public blockchain. This required both a technical whitepaper for developers and a business case narrative for C-level decision-makers.

International & multi-market GTM

For international GTM I add a localization layer beyond language: pricing model, channel, and cultural context. Pechat's Japan launch used retail partnerships (Tokyu Hands, Loft) and mom-blogger communities. The US launch shifted to e-commerce (Amazon) with English-language parenting influencers and a repositioned value prop ('the talking button for bedtime stories'). Same product, completely different story.

Real example

Datachain (IBC bridge): B2B institutional GTM — technical whitepaper + partner briefings + phased mainnet launch. Hakuhodo (Pechat): JP→US→EU expansion, +15% revenue in 12 months on US market, Good Design Award (グッドデザイン賞) recognition. Wargaming: represented World of Warships, World of Tanks, and World of Warplanes at Tokyo Game Show, PAX, and Gamescom — large-scale B2C GTM events with press, community, and partner coordination. SmartNews Ads: enterprise B2B GTM for advertiser acquisition — onboarding redesign contributed to +$2M/year revenue potential.
