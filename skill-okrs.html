OKRs

Setting goals that change behavior — from blockchain bridges to Ads programs.

What OKRs are for

OKRs are not a task list. They're a focus and alignment tool. A well-written OKR tells every team member: here's what matters this quarter, here's how we'll know if we succeeded, and here's how your work connects to the company direction. In Web3, where teams often juggle protocol work, community, business development, and product simultaneously, OKRs are the forcing function that prevents everything from being equally urgent.

Writing good OKRs

Objective: qualitative, inspiring, directional. 'Make the IBC bridge the most reliable cross-chain settlement layer in the Cosmos ecosystem.' Key Result: quantitative, binary at quarter end, and within team control. Bad KR: 'Improve bridge reliability.' Good KR: 'Achieve 99.9% transaction success rate on mainnet by end of Q3 as measured by on-chain monitoring dashboard.' In AdTech: 'Increase advertiser 30-day activation rate from 42% to 60% by end of Q3.'

OKRs in a Web3 context

Blockchain teams face a specific OKR challenge: much of the work is infrastructure and has indirect user impact. I define two OKR tracks: protocol-level KRs (transaction volume, chain pairs live, audit completion, testnet stability) and business-level KRs (partner integrations signed, institutional clients onboarded, developer ecosystem growth). Both matter — a technically perfect protocol with zero adoption is a failure.

The 70% rule and honest grading

OKRs should be set so 70% achievement is success. If a team consistently hits 100%, goals are too conservative. If they hit 40%, it's either ambition or capacity — and I review the trend to distinguish them. At SmartNews, the first OKR cycle scored 55% — not because the team underperformed but because the baseline was wrong. We recalibrated and hit 75% the next quarter. The learning was worth the uncomfortable first score.

Real example

SmartNews: introduced OKRs across 5 Ads teams as part of PDLC rollout. Weekly check-ins replaced monthly reporting — teams self-corrected within the quarter. Velocity +25% in 2 quarters. Datachain: defined protocol-level and business-level OKR tracks for the IBC bridge program — on-chain KPIs (transaction success rate, volume) alongside partner KPIs (integrations signed, institutional onboarding). The dual-track approach aligned Engineering (protocol focus) and BD (business focus) around shared success criteria.
