Experimentation Frameworks

Building a culture of learning at scale — AdTech, IoT, and beyond.

Beyond A/B tests

Experimentation at scale is an organizational capability, not a testing tool. It means every team can make evidence-based decisions quickly with a shared infrastructure, a common language, and a culture where a failed test is information, not a failure. I've built or adapted experimentation frameworks in AdTech (SmartNews), IoT consumer products (Hakuhodo/Pechat), and blockchain infrastructure (Datachain).

Framework components

1. Hypothesis backlog: prioritized bets ranked by expected impact and testability. 2. Experiment calendar: visibility on what's running, shipping, or in analysis. 3. Results repository: every experiment documented with setup, results, learnings, and follow-up. 4. Guardrail metrics: non-negotiable metrics that can never move negatively. In Web3/DeFi, guardrail metrics include on-chain reliability and smart contract security — no experiment can compromise protocol integrity.

Web3 experimentation specifics

In blockchain products, 'experimentation' has different constraints than SaaS. You can't A/B test smart contract logic on mainnet — you test on testnet first, then validate with a staged rollout. At Datachain, I applied a phased release model: testnet → limited mainnet (whitelisted partners) → full mainnet. Each phase had defined success criteria before the next gate opened. This is experimentation logic applied to protocol delivery.

Scaling across teams

At SmartNews, I rolled out PDLC (Product Development Lifecycle), DACI, and OKR frameworks across 5 Ads teams simultaneously. This reduced average time from hypothesis to test launch from 3 weeks to 5 days. Weekly experiment reviews replaced ad-hoc decisions. At Hakuhodo, I introduced Agile retrospectives as structured learning loops — not just process reviews but product hypothesis reviews, connecting sprint learnings to roadmap adjustments.

Real example

SmartNews: hypothesis-to-launch reduced from 3 weeks to 5 days across 5 teams. Hakuhodo (Pechat): experimentation on onboarding flow identified the activation step driving 3× D30 retention — directly shaped the 3-year roadmap. Datachain: phased mainnet release model (testnet → limited → full) applied as an experimentation framework for protocol delivery — 0 critical incidents on first IBC bridge mainnet launch.
