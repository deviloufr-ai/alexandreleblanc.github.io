Stakeholder Management

Turning alignment into momentum — from investors to protocol partners.

My approach

Stakeholder management is about building a coalition of people who understand the direction, trust the process, and feel heard even when the answer is no. In Web3, stakeholders include not just internal execs and teams but token holders, protocol partners, auditors, and community members — each with different information needs and influence levels.

Stakeholder mapping

High influence + high interest = manage closely, involve in decisions. High influence + low interest = keep informed, never surprise. In Web3 this means: protocol partners (high/high), investors and token holders (high/variable), community (medium/high). At Datachain I maintained separate communication channels for banking partners (formal weekly reports), internal leadership (concise weekly written update), and engineering teams (daily async Slack). At SmartNews I mapped 18 stakeholders across 5 teams and executive leadership in Japan and the US.

Managing up in a Web3 context

Web3 executives and investors expect data-backed confidence, not just effort reporting. I structure every exec update as: situation → complication → question → recommendation → ask. For blockchain-specific risks (audit delays, protocol dependency changes, chain governance votes), I present options with trade-offs rather than problems — and I surface issues early. A 3-week early warning on an audit delay is manageable; a same-week announcement destroys trust.

Managing external protocol partners

At Datachain, the IBC bridge required active coordination with Cosmos ecosystem partners and external chain maintainers. These are not employees — you can't mandate their timelines. I built relationship-first communication: regular technical syncs, shared dependency trackers, and early involvement in our release planning. When an external chain announced an unplanned upgrade, the relationship meant we had 3 weeks notice instead of 3 days.

Real example

Datachain: weekly stakeholder reporting to banking partners and leadership; satisfaction score 8/10 consistently. SmartNews: managed stakeholders across 5 Ads teams + Japan/US executive leadership; introduced weekly newsletter, quarterly OKR reviews, and a structured escalation path. Hakuhodo: navigated Japanese corporate stakeholder culture (nemawashi, ringi) for a 3-year roadmap and ¥100M R&D budget. Wargaming: coordinated between Tokyo HQ, regional marketing, PR, and support teams for TGS, PAX, and Gamescom launches.
