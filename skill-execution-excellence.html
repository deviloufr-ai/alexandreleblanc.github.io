How I work

Execution Excellence

Shipping what you said you'd ship — in Web3, on-chain, on time.

My definition

Execution excellence means shipping what you committed to, without burning the team. In Web3, the stakes are higher: a missed mainnet deployment has financial consequences, partner SLA implications, and community trust costs. I focus on removing blockers before they become blockers.

Sprint rhythm

Two-week sprints with a fixed, lean ceremony structure. Sprint goal written before backlog items are selected — the goal drives selection, not the other way around. Daily standups capped at 15 min: done / doing / blocked only. Mid-sprint Wednesday check to surface risks early. Retro + demo on sprint close Friday. At Datachain I ran this cadence across 3 teams on 2 timezones — Engineering (Tokyo), QA (mixed), and SRE/Ops (Europe).

Web3 execution specifics

Blockchain delivery adds layers of complexity absent in SaaS: smart contract freezes before audits, testnet validation cycles, on-chain transaction monitoring during deployment, and rollback constraints (you can't rollback a blockchain transaction). I built a pre-deployment checklist co-owned by Engineering, QA, and SRE — every IBC bridge release went through 47 checklist items before mainnet. Zero critical incidents over 12 months.

Risk management

I maintain a live risk register reviewed weekly. Each risk has an owner, likelihood, impact, and mitigation plan. I escalate proactively — a potential delay surfaced 3 weeks early is manageable; the same delay confirmed the day before a deadline is a crisis. At Datachain the risk register included protocol-level risks (IBC spec upgrades, Cosmos governance votes) alongside standard delivery risks.

Real example

At Datachain, I coordinated 3 cross-functional teams with 0 missed sprint goals over 8 consecutive sprints — after a period where previous sprints were consistently failing. At SmartNews, I managed 2 global Ads programs across 5 teams (18 people), boosting team velocity by 25% in 2 quarters through PDLC and DACI process rollouts. At Hakuhodo, Agile adoption reduced average feature delivery time by 40%.
