How I work

Execution Excellence

Delivery without chaos

My definition

Execution excellence means shipping what you said you'd ship, on time, without burning out the team. It's about removing blockers before they become blockers, not managing fires after they start.

Sprint rhythm

I run two-week sprints with a fixed ceremony structure: sprint planning on Monday morning (60 min max), daily standups capped at 15 min with a strict format (done / doing / blocked), mid-sprint check on Wednesday to surface risks early, and a retro + demo on Friday of sprint close. No ceremony bloat — if a meeting doesn't change a decision, it shouldn't exist.

Risk management

I maintain a live risk register updated weekly. Each risk has an owner, a likelihood score, an impact score, and a mitigation plan. I escalate proactively — I'd rather surface a potential delay 3 weeks early than confirm a miss the day before a deadline.

Metrics I track

Velocity (moving average over 6 sprints), sprint goal achievement rate (target: >85%), escaped defect rate, and time-to-close on P1 bugs. I review these with the team monthly — not to police performance, but to have an honest conversation about capacity and quality.

Real example

At Datachain, I coordinated 3 cross-functional teams with 0 missed sprint goals over 8 consecutive sprints — after a period where previous sprints were consistently failing. The key was async-first communication: all decisions documented in Confluence within 24h, all blockers surfaced in a shared Slack channel with a 4-hour SLA for response.
